Website Statistics, Web Analytics and Traffic Stats 2012 Review

As defined by the Official WAA, web analytics is the collection, measurement, reporting and analysis of web based data in order to understand and optimize website usage. There are two generally accepted categories of web analytics (and statistics); off-site and on-site. As its name denotes, off-site analytics and statistics includes the measurement, analysis and reporting of a website's potential audience (opportunity), share of voice (visibility), and buzz (comments) that is occuring on the Internet as a whole.

On-site analytics refers specifically to the web based measurement, analysis and reporting of specific data on a given website. This includes conversion elements (i.e. landing pages, exit pages, etc.) On-site analytics measures the web based performance of a website from a commercial perspective. When "web analytics" is used in a commercial context most people are refering to on-site web analytics.

If you're looking for website statistics, free web stats or analytics to help you better understand your website's traffic then you've come to the right place.     • Google Analytics
    So why do we recommend Google web analytics? Well first of all its free! So you want to know Whats the catch? It may sound too good to be true, but in fact Google does provide a very high quality free web analytics and statistics solution for small businesses. However, many people in the Internet world speculate how Google might be exploiting this free offering to benefit their own business objectives. Many businesses wonder what confidential information Google is collecting via their free analytics service. What will they do will all this data they are collecting? Other people want to know if Google positioning itself to become the next technology empire, second only to Microsoft? It really is difficult to say. Notwithstanding their personal agenda we have to admit Google Analytics is a great web analytics tool and it may be just what you're looking for.

    For those interested in Google Analytics you should know that Google Analytics provides three reporting views: Webmaster, Marketer and Executive. Each reporting view provides the user a unique sets of reports. The views are offered as an easy way to access and organize other basic reports. We think that Google Analytics really is a great web stats program.

    Are there any disadvantages to using Google Analytics? We think the biggest drawback to using Google Analytics is that it doesn't appear to provide real time statistics. If you want real time stats this might be reason enough to use a fee based web analytics program. We also think that Google Analytics may have some problems with the accuracy of their stats - but the verdict is still out on this one. The reality is that any two web analytics programs will likely vary slightly in the figures they provide. Finally, there is some concern by skeptics that Google will use the data they collect to provide better advertising relevancy to their advertising customers. The concern has been raised by a few bloggers that ultimately you could be helping your competition by providing Google with your conversion data.
